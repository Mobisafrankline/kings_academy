Primary Education

This is the foundational level of education in Kenya and is compulsory for all children. It typically spans eight years, starting at the age of six or seven. The primary education curriculum covers subjects such as English, mathematics, science, social studies, and languages.

Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC)

The Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) is an education system that was introduced in Kenya as a reform to the previous 8-4-4 system. The CBC focuses on the development of competencies in learners rather than solely emphasizing academic knowledge

The history of computers

The history of computers is a remarkable journey of human ingenuity and innovation spanning several centuries. It all started with ancient tools like the abacus and mechanical calculators, gradually leading to the development of electronic computers using vacuum tubes, transistors, and microprocessors. These milestones laid the groundwork for personal computers, graphical user interfaces (GUI), and the internet, revolutionizing the way we interact with technology. The 20th century witnessed groundbreaking advancements in electronic computing. The invention of vacuum tubes in the 1930s and 1940s enabled the creation of early electronic computers, which played a crucial role in World War II and beyond. Transistors emerged in 1947, replacing vacuum tubes and revolutionizing computer design by offering enhanced speed, reliability, and miniaturization. The 1970s and 1980s brought about a computing revolution with the advent of personal computers like the Altair 8800, Apple I and II, and Commodore PET, making computing accessible to individuals and small businesses. Additionally, Xerox PARC's Alto computer introduced GUI and the mouse, significantly improving user-friendliness. In the 1990s, the Internet and the World Wide Web transformed global communication and information sharing, connecting people worldwide. The 2000s and 2010s witnessed the rise of mobile computing, cloud computing, and big data technologies, further shaping modern technology. As we look ahead, artificial intelligence holds tremendous potential to revolutionize various industries and daily life, while quantum computing promises unparalleled computational power. The history of computers stands as a testament to human creativity and persistence, driving us towards an exciting future of limitless possibilities.
